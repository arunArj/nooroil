Lemon-Thyme
Chicken Roast

Lebanese

60 minutes
500 Kcal
4 people

Ingredients

  • Whole roasting chicken (Around 3.26kg)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter at room temperature
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme and a few sprigs (2-4)
  • 8 thin lemon slices
  • 1 tbsp Noor Oil (or half a cup)
  • 1 cup yogurt (optional)
  • 2 cups chicken stock or broth
  • 2 tbsp all purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle the chicken inside out with 2 tbsp salt. Put into a plastic bag and refrigerate for at least 16 hours.
  2. Remove the chicken from the fridge 2 hours before roasting.
  3. Preheat Oven to 220 C.
  4. In a bowl mix together the butter and minced thyme. Slip your fingers underneath the chicken skin and loosen it all over and massage the butter under the skin.
  5. Slip the lemon slices under the breast skin, 4 slices on each side.
  6. Rub the chicken all over with yogurt (optional) and Noor Oil, and season with 0.5 tsp pepper.
  7. Place a large, ovenproof pan in the oven for 5 minutes until it is very hot. Meanwhile, twist a 30cm length of aluminum foil into a rope, and fashion it into a ring and place it in the center of the pan.
  8. Place the chicken breast side up on top of the ring and return the pan into the oven, basting occasionally with the fat in the pan, for about 1 hour.
  9. Let it rest for 15 minutes before carving it.
  10. Pour the pan drippings into a heatproof measuring cup. Let stand, then spoon off and reserve the fat. Add the stock to the drippings left in the cup.
  11. Add 2 tbsp of the fat to a frying pan and whisk in the flour. Gradually whisk in the stock mixture and the cream and bring to a boil. Simmer while whisking until it thickens.
  12. Season with salt and pepper, add the thyme sprigs.

Notes

  • You can skip step 2.
  • You can add a tablespoon of Dijon mustard.
